George Dawson (b.1821 - d.1876)

Preacher. Lecturer. Activist.

The person who married my great-great grandfather, Philip Henry Furney, and great-great-grandmother leading them to give their next born son, George Dawson Furney (my great-grandfather), the middlename Dawson, thereafter becoming the defacto middle name for all descendant Furneys since.

Timeline

  • 24 Feb 1821George Dawson is born in Brunswick Square, London.
  • Sep 1840Philip Henry Furney is born in Glasgow, Scotland, second born child and first born son of parents Philip Henry Furney and Jane Furney. His father Philip is an Excise Officer for the Inland Revenue, originally from Monmouthshire. The family is recorded as briefly living in Glasgow, in Crawford Street, where their son Philip is born.
  • 1841George Dawson graduates from the University of Glasgow.
  • 1843He joins the pastorate of the Baptist Church at Rickmansworth.
  • 1844He moves to Birmingham and joins the Mount Zion Baptist Church where "his eloquence and beliefs attract a large following".
  • 1845Soon after he leaves the Baptist Church with the majority of his congregation in tow to become minister of the Church of the Saviour, a Unitarian church erected specifically for his liberal views. Here he develops the concept of the Civic Gospel based on improving life for all citizens. His sermons are said to be unconventional for the time, electrifying the Birmingham public, preaching as a living man to living men who found life no simple or easy matter. Dawson did not actually consider himself to be a Unitarian, but instead free of any definite creed or doctrinal rigidity. He believed true religion was "social, unitive, and brotherly in its spirit". He was famouse during his life time for lecturing on a wide range of subjects from Shakespeare to German poetry, Italian history to good etiquette.
  • 1871George Dawson becomes editor of the Birmingham Morning News. He employes the first woman reporter, Maria Beauclerc, who also has outstanding shorthand skills. Her shorthand notes of his sermons later form the basis of the books of his sermons which are published after this death by editor and minister, George St. Clair.
  • 29 Mar 1872Philip Henry Furney marries Lizzie Swaine at the Church of the Saviour in Edward Street in the district of Birmingham in the County of Warwick. Philip Henry is 31 years old, a widower and a draper, living at 25 New Street Birmingham. Lizzie Swaine is 19 years old, a spinster, living at 14 Gough Road, Birmingham. They are married by the minister George Dawson, in the presence of Letitia Kelsey and Owen David Lawrence.
  • 28 Feb 1873Lizzie gives birth to their son (first?) who they name Stanley Bernard.
  • 1875A (second?) son is born, this time named after his father, Philip Henry.
  • 19 Sep 1875Philip Henry Furney is christened at the Church of the Saviour by George Dawson. The family is recorded as living in Mary Street, Balsall Heath.
  • 30 Nov 1876George Dawson dies suddenly at King's Norton, near Birmingham.
  • June 1880A further son is born to Philip and Lizzie and they name him George Dawson. (Philip and Lizzie will give birth to a further two sons, Henry Charles and Ernest H N, before Philip remarries and has a further four children with his third wife.)
  • 17 Oct 1880George Dawson Furney is christened by minister George St. Clair, a former assistant to George Dawson. The family is recorded as living at 157 Varna Road, Birmingham.
  • 1880Sculptor Thomas Woolner creates a statue of George Dawson which until recently stood in Chamberlain Square, Birmingham (now in storage).
  • 1889Birmingham becomes a City, and in 1890 an American observer describes it as “the best governed city in the world”.
  • 16 Jun 1907George Dawson Furney marries Mabel Rose Atwater at the parish church of St. Johns in Clapham, London.
  • 28 Aug 1913George and Mabel give birth to a son who they name Lewis George Dawson Furney, thereby continuing what will become a line of Dawson Furneys, one of whom at least curious to know "where the Dawson came from".
